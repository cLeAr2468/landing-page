PIT Vision, Mission & LRC Goals

Guiding principles and objectives of the Polytechnic State University and its Learning Resource Center.

VISION

A premier polytechnic state university.

MISSION

To strengthen its capacity of producing highly-skilled, competent, and world-class human resources, responsible to fulfill stakeholder needs, goals of national development and ASEAN integration for the attainment of a good quality of life with the guidance and providence of God.

PIT LRC GOAL

To provide appropriate and timely information with maximum service through the use of the materials in the learning resource center, thereby providing for the fulfillment of the clientele’s educational, cultural, professional, aesthetical, recreational research needs in support to PIT’s vision and mission.

OBJECTIVES

  1. Provide a wide variety of information resources that support, enrich and satisfy the curricula and research needs of the College.
  2. Upgrade and strengthen the resources and existing facilities and equipment to meet the research needs of the clientele.
  3. Develop student’s awareness of the value of the resources in the Learning Resource Center as a means for lifelong education and provide bibliographic tool to the contents of the collection.
  4. Encourage faculty members in promoting the effective use of the resources by the students and for them to utilize more fully for their professional growth and for the improvement of the institute.
  5. Update operations and services in the Learning Resource Center and investigate the changing educational needs and to provide access to information, programs and services.
  6. Establish linkages with local, national and international institutions to enhance library facilities and resources.

Policies & Rules

General Rules

  1. Observe Silence. Idle conversation, loud laughter, and other unnecessary conversation must be avoided. Talking out of necessity must be done in a subdued tone.
  2. Eating, smoking, gambling, and vandalism are not allowed in the library.
  3. Books in the reading area should not be returned to the shelves but instead, these should be left on the table after reading to avoid their being misplaced.
    Exception: books belonging to a set of volumes, such as encyclopedias, should be returned to their proper shelves.
  4. The library should be kept clean and orderly. Chairs must be pushed back under the table before leaving the library.
Note: Students who habitually break the rules will be punished accordingly.
1st Offense: Reprimand
2nd Offense: Shall be subjected to suspension of library privileges for not more than two weeks.

Control Procedures

A. Entrance Checking

  1. Every library clientele should present his/her school ID upon entering the library and should login their name in the attendance sheet shall be worn while inside the library.
  2. Visitors from other schools or agencies should deposit his/her ID and a Letter of Request signed by the Head of Office or their Librarian should they want to avail the library resources. A minimal fee of PhP 50.00 shall be charged per visitor and to be paid at the Cahier’s Office.
    Note: No visitor shall be allowed to take out any resources/materials from the library.

B. Exit Checking

  1. Upon leaving the library, every user is required to:
  2. Open the bag, folder or big envelope and show its contents.
  3. Show the date due slip of every library material that has to be taken out.

Lost Book

  1. When a book is lost, the borrower must report the matter immediately to the librarian; otherwise, if the book becomes overdue, the borrower will have to pay the overdue fine incurred until the day the loss is reported.
  2. The lost book can be replaced if the exact title, author, and copyright date is available or it can be replaced with the current edition. If the exact title is not available in the market, a similar title or subject matter can be replaced with a lost book and with a processing fee of 200.

Fines

  1. A fine is a monetary penalty imposed to borrowers who fail to return the borrowed library materials on the date stipulated on the Date Due Slip. Fine is computed by counting the number of days from the day after the book is due up to the day the book is being returned.
  2. When a book is not returned on or before 8:30 A.M. on the due date given, the book is already considered “overdue” book.
  3. Schedule of Fines: 10 pesos in all types of reference materials, counting of days of fines includes weekends and holidays.

Book Drop

  1. A service provided to the library patron to return their loaned/borrowed books if the library is closed, or the borrower is in a hurry for his/her class. The borrower may just drop the book/s borrowed in the Book Drop. The library personnel will check the box three (3) times a day during office hours. Once you drop the book(s) this will be checked-in into the system. If the book is already overdue by the time the book is being dropped, the borrower will be notified to settle his/her overdue book(s).
