Policies & Rules

Guidelines and regulations that ensure a conducive learning environment for all users.

General Rules

  1. Observe Silence. Idle conversation, loud laughter, and other unnecessary conversation must be avoided. Talking out of necessity must be done in a subdued tone.
  2. Eating, smoking, gambling, and vandalism are not allowed in the library.
  3. Books in the reading area should not be returned to the shelves but instead, these should be left on the table after reading to avoid their being misplaced.
    Exception: books belonging to a set of volumes, such as encyclopedias, should be returned to their proper shelves.
  4. The library should be kept clean and orderly. Chairs must be pushed back under the table before leaving the library.

Note:

Students who habitually break the rules will be punished accordingly.
1st Offense: Reprimand 2nd Offense: Shall be subjected to suspension of library privileges for not more than two weeks.

Control Procedures

A. Entrance Checking

  1. Every library clientele should present his/her school ID upon entering the library and should login their name in the attendance sheet shall be worn while inside the library.
  2. Visitors from other schools or agencies should deposit his/her ID and a Letter of Request signed by the Head of Office or their Librarian should they want to avail the library resources. A minimal fee of PhP 50.00 shall be charged per visitor and to be paid at the Cahier’s Office.

Note: No visitor shall be allowed to take out any resources/materials from the library.

B. Exit Checking

  1. Upon leaving the library, every user is required to:
  2. Open the bag, folder or big envelope and show its contents.
  3. Show the date due slip of every library material that has to be taken out.

Lost Book

When a book is lost, the borrower must report the matter immediately to the librarian; otherwise, if the book becomes overdue, the borrower will have to pay the overdue fine incurred until the day the loss is reported.

The lost book can be replaced if the exact title, author, and copyright date is available or it can be replaced with the current edition. If the exact title is not available in the market, a similar title or subject matter can be replaced with a lost book and with a processing fee of 200.

Fines

A fine is a monetary penalty imposed to borrowers who fail to return the borrowed library materials on the date stipulated on the Date Due Slip. Fine is computed by counting the number of days from the day after the book is due up to the day the book is being returned.

When a book is not returned on or before 8:30 A.M. on the due date given, the book is already considered “overdue” book.

Schedule of Fines:

10 pesos in all types of reference materials, counting of days of fines includes weekends and holidays.

Book Drop

A service provided to the library patron to return their loaned/borrowed books if the library is closed, or the borrower is in a hurry for his/her class. The borrower may just drop the book/s borrowed in the Book Drop. The library personnel will check the box three (3) times a day during office hours. Once you drop the book(s) this will be checked-in into the system. If the book is already overdue by the time the book is being dropped, the borrower will be notified to settle his/her overdue book(s).
